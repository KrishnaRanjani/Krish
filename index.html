About

            I work as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. My research focuses on advancing personalized medical care by integrating machine learning methodologies and large-scale biomedical data analysis. My research lies at the intersection of multi-agent reinforcement learning, graph neural networks, control theory, physical simulation, and augmented reality. Specifically, I am currently applying medical imaging and AI in healthcare to monitor and predict cancer cell behaviour. Beyond academia, I serve as CEO of the Madras Scientific Research Foundation (MSRF), aiming to democratize education and research opportunities, especially for underprivileged students globally. During my academic journey, I conducted pioneering research in Natural Language Processing (NLP) for Law Prediction in Cybercriminal Activity, culminating in my Master's thesis under the guidance of Prof.Sathish Kumar. Furthermore, as an undergraduate I undertook a project to enhance environmental monitoring, utilizing Rover technology under the mentorship of Prof. Nithya
